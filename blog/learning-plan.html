Learning Plan

Bootcamp

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My mom always told me growing up that I should do what I love. I'm not sure I really love to do anything. I'm enjoying learning about coding & web-development right now through this course and to me, that's enough.

I'm at the point where I'm still trying to pin-point which parts of coding are fun/enjoyable for me. When I figure that out, I think I'll know where to go from there.

My long-term plan is to do what I want & take it a day at a time. My long-term goal is to be secure & happy.


What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

The biggest strength I have approaching bootcamp is my perspective on 'allowing'.

After a month of being a full-time student, with nothing else really going on in my life, the importance of community and support has really presented itself to me.

I've been making more of an effort to go out and visit my family, make plans with friends. I've been trying to interact with my peers, neighbours & strangers whenever the opportuinities arise.

I've become more open & have allowed myself to be more vulnerable & honest with others. I suppose, in the same kind of way, I've allowed myself to be more vulnerable & honest with myself too.

I can admit when I feel frustrated or when I'm struggling. These days instead of shaming myself, I just sit & allow these feelings to pass.

The biggest limitation I think I'll face during bootcamp is perfectionism.

"Perfectionism is the belief or desire that everything should be flawless or done perfectly, often leading to high standards that are difficult to achieve and stress when they're not met."

Through out foundations, I've been able to learn more about myself and one of the things I've learnt is I'm a bit of a perfectionist. If I'm not naturally or immediately good at things, I get extremely frustrated.

I tend to be quite hard on myself & when I reflect back on my time learning, I find myself wishing I'd done things differently/more efficiently, even though I know in my head that I was only doing what I thought/knew was best at the time.


What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Impostor Syndrome. I feel like I'm quite prone to belittling myself. Whether it be my ambitions or efforts, I have a bad habit of making myself feel unworthy.

I can't believe I'm admitting this in a school blog but I think I have low self-esteem. I suppose this is where a-lot of my issues are rooted.


What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to leave with a better self-esteem.


What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

My feelings haven't changed since the last Learning Plan entry I completed for foundations. I'll copy and paste it below for reference.

I don't really expect anything from the Facilitation team. I'll always ask for help if I ever need it, but should the Facilitation team ever think I could benefit with some constructive criticism or whatever, by all means.


What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

Success.
