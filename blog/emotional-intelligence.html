The Importance of Emotional Intelligence

EQ vs IQ

We're gonna be looking at two acronyms today, EQ and IQ. Before discussing them, we'll need to know the differences between them:

EQ

  • Emotional quotient
  • Measure of emotional competency
  • Involves identifying, controlling, and using emotions
  • Affects motivation, empathy, relationships, self-awareness, and self-control

IQ

  • Intelligence quotient
  • Measure of cognitive abilities
  • Involves processing, knowledge, memory, and reasoning
  • Affects academics, expertise, critical thinking, and logic

The foundation of EQ:

  • Self Awareness
  • Self Management
  • Social Awareness
  • Relationship Management

Being human is awfully confusing

Each person is wired & built differently, no one is one and the same; the only rule is we're alive. As our lives transpire, we live to become more and more complicated. Funnily enough, often when we're asked what we want to be, a common answer is "Happy." Having a high EQ can help you live a happier life.

There is an art to getting along with other people. There is a large array of differences that separates you from the people around you.

  • Having a low EQ might look like: Doing what's easiest & gravitating towards people who are very similar & alike to yourself - evading those who are 'too different' from you and those who you disagree with.
  • Having a high EQ might look like: Accepting not everyone is for you, just like you're not for everyone. Approaching those you do not agree with, with an open-mind, from a place of curiousity, with an intention to understand.

When you refuse to step into discomfort, you rob yourself of an authentic human experience. When we have the ability to look outside of ourselves and to see ourselves & other people within the bigger picture, we not only gain the ability to coexist with others harmoniously but the ability to question & to grow.

A friendly reminder that:

It takes time, it takes effort. Just like everything you've ever worked for in your life, emotional intelligence is not something you've inherited. It is an active choice & requires work. It requires practice, it requires patience.

Just as you can recount your life & appreciate how big and vast your experience has been - so can everyone else. There is a lot to be gained & a lot to love in the world outside of yourself. In saying this, as much as you should respect others, you should also respect yourself.

Check in with yourself

It's important to be in-tune with our environment & to be self-aware in how we treat others. On the flip-side it's also important, if not more so, to be in-tune with ourselves. We are not machines or robots; there isn't an infinite resource for us to be able to tap into & take from.

We get hungry, thirsty, tired. Sometimes we don't have the capacity to lend an ear to a friend in need. We don't have the capacity to be as social as we'd like. We don't have the capacity to go do over-time again at work. We have needs and we have a duty to listen to them.

Emotional intelligence looks like, being your own priority. Knowing where you yourself stand. Spending time with yourself, by yourself. You are the one greatest narrator of your own life.

Personally,

I am someone who believes that all things require balance. There is a difference between being content and moving forwards because of it & being content and staying where you are. Place yourself into whatever makes you uncomfortable, jump into the unknown, seek to learn and to grow.

You shouldn't dwell in places like the past of the future for too long - be in the present. If you're gonna dwell in the past, reflect back on what you've learnt reading this blog entry.
