EQ vs IQ

We're gonna be looking at two acronyms today, EQ and IQ. Before discussing them, we'll need to know the differences between them:

EQ

  • Emotional quotient
  • Measure of emotional competency
  • Involves identifying, controlling, and using emotions
  • Affects motivation, empathy, relationships, self-awareness, and self-control

IQ

  • Intelligence quotient
  • Measure of cognitive abilities
  • Involves processing, knowledge, memory, and reasoning
  • Affects academics, expertise, critical thinking, and logic

The Importance of Emotional Intelligence

Being human is awfully confusing. Each person is wired & built differently, no one is one and the same; the only rule is we live and as our life transpires, we live to become more and more complicated.

There is an art to getting along with other people. There is a large array of differences that separates you from the people around you. You may find yourself doing what's easiest & gravitate towards people who are very similar & alike to yourself.

I think when you refuse to step into discomfort & you refuse your own curiousities, you rob yourself of an authentic human experience. When we have the ability to look outside of ourselves and to see ourselves & other people within the bigger picture, we gain the ability to coexist with other people harmoniously. It's important to note, this is an active choice & requires work.

Just like everything you've ever worked for in your life, emotional intelligence is not something you've inherited. It's something that you've worked on, regardless of whether you've done this intentionally or not. Emotional intelligence is a lifestyle, it's something that requires maintenance. If you don't attune yourself to practicing what you've learnt, you're prone to losing it - forgetting it.

It's important to remember, just as you can recount your life & appreciate how big and vast your experience has been - so can everyone else. There is a lot to be gained & a lot to love in the world outside of yourself. This also applies going inwards. Just as much as you should respect others, you should also respect yourself.

As important as it is to be in-tune with our environment & to be self-aware in how we treat others, it is also important for us to have a desire to be selfish. We are not machines or robots; there isn't an infinite resource for us to be able to tap into & take from. We get hungry, thirsty, tired. Sometimes we don't have the capacity to lend an ear to a friend, we don't have the capacity to be as social as we'd like, we don't have the capacity to go do over-time again at work. We have needs and we have a duty to listen to them as we are our own keepers.

Emotional intelligence also looks like, being your own priority. Knowing where you yourself stand. Spending time with yourself, taking care of yourself, speaking with yourself. You are the one greatest narrator of your own life. You shouldn't dwell in places like the past of the future for too long - be in the present, with yourself, by yourself.

I am someone who believes that all things require balance. I am also someone who believes that all things require a solid foundation. As someone who holds these beliefs, I feel it's of utmost importance for everyone to be striving to be more emotionally intelligent.
