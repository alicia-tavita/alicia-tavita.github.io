Identity Values Questions & Answers:

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I am the eldest daughter of an ethnic household, born into expectations & responsibility. My whole childhood & teenage life consisted of nothing but cleaning & working. For the most part, I quietly assumed my job as my families keeper & my mothers right hand.

When I was 17 I cussed at my mother for the very first time, shoved my clothes into rubbish bags and ran away. The choice I'd made was not at all well thought out or smart. It was a split second decision, driven by emotion. I had no plans, no direction & no future - though no one really runs away from home unless they're being chased.

When I think about it now, the only regret I have is not taking that step sooner. My life begun when I made the choice to leave & when I made the choice to jump into the unknown. I wish I didn't spend most of my life silently accepting & enduring.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I've spent a lot of my life in grief, mourning for the experiences/upbringing I felt I'd deserved & was robbed of. In hindsight, I'm grateful I've come out the other side, the person I am today.

I've become someone who actively strives to be better. Shifting perspective/s, questioning everything, reflecting consistently/constantly - these practices are important to me. I have a desire to live my life fully, present in all of its good and bad. To live auto-pilot in contentment, is the same as death to me.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I've worked in retail for nearly 10 years now. The experience & skills I've been able to gain from being in the work force from a young age, has been valuable.

  • Communication & articulation. When working in a team, it's important you're able to communicate effectively & efficiently with each other.
  • Taking intiative. You can't wait for others to tell you what you need to hear, you can't wait for the world to "give you a sign". Ask yourself the relevant questions to improve and ask yourself regularly.
  • Resilience. I've worked in many fast pace work environments, I've become someone who can work well under pressure.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

My downfall is my laziness. I've spent a majority of my life in a state of hyper vigilance. So I've stepped into my young adult years & I'm all burnt out. It's hard for me to keep the bigger picture in mind when in the present, I'm exhausted.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

It's so cliche. I was in university and it was my very first group project. I was working with two other people who basically just refused to work. Ultimately they contributed nothing & ultimately I came to the conclusion that it was out of my control.

I decided to take initiative with the project. Instead of seeing this situation as "This is so unfair, I can't believe I'm stuck doing this whole project by myself", I re-shifted perspectives and began to see it as "It's a blessing to have full control over this project. I decide the outcome of this situation".

The day before the presentation, I met with my group & I presented them with scripts, detailing everything they needed to learn. Presentation day came, we presented. The results came, we got full marks. In hindsight, I wish I'd had the courage to speak with my professor & to present my presentation by myself.
